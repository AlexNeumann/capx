About CIS 440

Course Description and Objectives

CIS 440, aka "Enterprise Systems Development", is the capstone course for the W. P. Carey Schoolâ€™s undergraduate Information Systems program. Over the course of fifteen weeks (or in the summer semester, six very compressed weeks), our senior students work together in self-organizing teams to complete IT/IS projects that deliver tangible business value to real businesses. They apply the whole range of skills they have acquired through earlier Information Systems coursework, internships, and work experience.

Some of the important topics we cover in the class, and that students can immediately put to practical use, include:

  • the Agile approach to project management, and the Scrum framework in particular
  • project scoping and requirements discovery
  • metrics and "information radiators" for empirical project management
  • modern software development practices including test-driven-development (TDD)
  • Dev/Ops, continuous integration, automated testing, and continuous delivery

One of the main objectives of the course is that students will improve their communication and organizing skills as they learn from (brutal) experience how to work as a self-organizing team and engage with a real client to complete a project with limited time and resources. They invariably experience miscommunications, unpredictably changing requirements, and a host of other challenges, and learn to overcome them.
